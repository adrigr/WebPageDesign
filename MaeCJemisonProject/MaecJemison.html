Biography

Carrer

Mae Jemison is a historicaly influential women because of the fact that she was the first african-american AND female astronaut. Not only was she the first female astronaut, she was the first women in space. On September 12, 1992 she was aboard the Endeavour on misson STS47. Jemison started her impressive carrer with securing a medical degree and working as a general practitioner. Then she was the medical officer for the area Peace Corps (In Sierra Leone and Liberia), she also taught and did medical reasearch there. When she got back to the U.S in 1985, she decided to make a carrer change. She applied to NASA's astronaut training program. After her carrer as an astronaut, she taught at Dartmouth College.


Family

Jemison is the youngest child out of her 2 other siblings. Her sister, Ada Jemison Bulloc became a child psychiatrist. Her brother, Charles Jemison became a real estate broker. Her father, Charlie Jemison, was a roofer and carpenter. Her mother, Dorothy (Green) Jemison, was an elementary school teacher. Mae Jemison never married or had children. Her father, Charlie Jemison died of a heart attack at 78. Her mother, Dorothy (Green) Jemison died at 64 in Ingalls Hospital.

Achievments

Mae Jemison was recongnized for her accomplishments through awards. She recieved several honorary doctorates including...


  • 1988 Essence Science and Technology Award
  • 1992 Ebony Black Achievment Award
  • 1993 Montgomery Fellowship (from Dartmouth College)

Jemison also was named Gamma Sigma Gamma Woman of the Year in 1990. Also, in 1992, the Mae C. Jemison Academy, an alternative public school in Michigan, was named after her.


Jemison was not only awarded and celebrated for all her achievments, she also gave back to the community by being a member of several promiet organizations, including...


  • American Medical Association
  • American Chemical Society
  • American Association for the Advancement of Science
  • World Sickle Cell Foundation
  • American Express Geography Competition
  • Center for the Prevention of Childhood Malnutrition
